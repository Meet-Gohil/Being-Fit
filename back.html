Back Exercises

Pull-Ups

Description:

Strengthens the upper back, including the latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, and traps. Enhances grip strength and overall upper body endurance. How to Perform

How to Perform:

  • 1.Hang from a pull-up bar with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, palms facing away from you.
  • 2.Engage your core and pull your body upward until your chin is above the bar.
  • 3.Slowly lower yourself back to the starting position.

    • Bent-Over Rows

    Description:

    Targets the middle back, including the lats, rhomboids, and traps. Improves posture and back strength. How to Perform

    How to Perform:

  • 1.Stand with your feet hip-width apart and bend your knees slightly.
  • 2.Hinge at the hips and keep your back flat, holding a barbell or dumbbells with an overhand grip.
  • 3.Pull the weight towards your lower ribcage, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  • 4.Lower the weight back to the starting position.

    • Deadlifts

    Description:

    Engages the entire back, especially the lower back, along with the hamstrings, glutes, and core. Builds overall strength and improves functional fitness. How to Perform

    How to Perform:

  • 1.Stand with your feet hip-width apart, holding a barbell with an overhand grip in front of your thighs.
  • 2.Engage your core and keep your back straight as you hinge at the hips and lower the barbell towards the ground.
  • 3.Keep the barbell close to your body and lower it to mid-shin level.
  • 4.Push through your heels to return to the starting position.

    • Lat Pulldowns

    Description:

    Focuses on the latissimus dorsi and upper back. Helps improve back width and overall upper body strength. How to Perform

    How to Perform:

  • 1.Sit at a lat pulldown machine and grasp the bar with a wide grip, palms facing away from you.
  • 2.Pull the bar down towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  • 3.Slowly return the bar to the starting position with controlled movement.

    • Face Pulls

    Description:

    Strengthens the upper back, rear deltoids, and rotator cuffs. Enhances shoulder stability and posture. How to Perform

    How to Perform:

  • 1.Set up a cable machine with a rope attachment at upper chest level.
  • 2.Grasp the rope with an overhand grip and step back to create tension in the cable.
  • 3.Pull the rope towards your face, keeping your upper arms parallel to the ground and elbows flared out.
  • 4.Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of the movement, then slowly return to the starting position.

    • Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows

    Description:

    Isolates and strengthens the lats, rhomboids, and traps. Helps improve unilateral strength and balance. How to Perform

    How to Perform:

  • 1.Place one knee and one hand on a bench for support, keeping your back flat.
  • 2.Hold a dumbbell in the opposite hand with your arm extended towards the floor.
  • 3.Pull the dumbbell towards your hip, squeezing your shoulder blade at the top.
  • 4.Slowly lower the weight back to the starting position.

    • T-Bar Rows

    Description:

    Targets the middle back, including the lats, rhomboids, and traps. Builds thickness and strength in the back. How to Perform

    How to Perform:

    :
  • 1.Stand over a T-bar row machine or set up a barbell with one end secured.
  • 2.Grasp the handles or the barbell with both hands, keeping your chest up and back flat.
  • 3.Pull the weight towards your lower ribcage, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  • 4.Lower the weight back to the starting position.
